### Tier 8 ‚Äî Counterparty Credit Risk (CCR) Integration (NEW 2026-05-21; reordered 2026-05-22 for first-batch execution)

CCR is a separate-but-adjacent risk type to default credit risk: it produces the **EAD** for derivative and SFT (securities financing transaction) exposures, which then flows through the existing SA / IRB risk-weight machinery to produce the counterparty default-risk RWA. ### P8 Tier 8 ‚Äî CCR Integration (long-form roadmap)

- **Status:** [ ] Not started ‚Äî new epic added 2026-05-21
- **Total effort:** L‚ÄìXL across 30+ tickets, sequenced over 6‚Äì9 months for SA-CCR + CVA; IMM deferred to v2.0 (+6 months).
- **Headline rationale:** the calculator currently has no representation of derivative or SFT exposures. Trade-level data, netting sets, and margin agreements have no schema; the pipeline has no stage that produces an EAD from those inputs. Without CCR, the calculator cannot produce a complete RWA number for any firm with non-trivial derivative activity. SA-CCR is the regulatory floor method post-CRR2 and is mandatory under PRA PS1/26 for all firms above the small-derivative-portfolio threshold (Art. 273a). CVA capital is a separable but adjacent epic that depends on SA-CCR EAD.

#### Scope decisions (operator-confirmed 2026-05-22)

1. **Simplified SA-CCR (Art. 281) + OEM (Art. 282) ‚Äî deferred to v2.0 / next-major-epic.** Not in initial epic. Sub-threshold portfolios fall through to full SA-CCR (always conservative; no capital understatement risk). P8.21 and P8.22 moved to Phase 10.
2. **CVA epic ‚Äî BA-CVA only initially.** P8.60 (BA-CVA), P8.62 (hedges eligibility), P8.63 (aggregated CVA on bundle) stay in scope. **P8.61 (SA-CVA) deferred to v2.0** (moved to Phase 10).
3. **IMM ‚Äî confirmed deferred to v2.0.** Model-governance footprint disproportionate to scope. P8.90 / P8.91 in Phase 10.
4. **SFTs ‚Äî route through CCR with `risk_type=CCR_SFT`, reusing existing CRM haircut tables in `engine/crm/`.** No duplication of the supervisory haircut table. The dual `risk_type` routing is already encompassed by existing tickets (P8.5 schemas extend `risk_type`; P8.20 pipeline integration; P8.30 SA routing) ‚Äî no new P-code needed.

#### Risk register

- **R-CCR-1** ‚Äî Trade-level data is a step-change. Existing fixtures are exposure-level. Mitigation: build minimal CCR-A1 (one trade, one netting set, unmargined IR swap) in Phase 1 alongside loader work, not at the end.
- **R-CCR-2** ‚Äî Netting-set group-by is a new performance class. Polars handles this well, but every per-trade column ‚Üí per-netting-set aggregation is a shuffle. Mitigation: benchmark from CCR-A2 onward, not at the end.
- **R-CCR-3** ‚Äî Supervisory delta for options requires Œ¶(d1). Already have `polars-normal-stats` in deps (used by IRB). Verify dependency covers our needs.
- **R-CCR-4** ‚Äî Œ±=1.4 is regulator-default; some firms have PRA permission for firm-specific Œ±. Config-driven from day 1 (P8.6) avoids retrofitting.
- **R-CCR-5** ‚Äî COREP C 34.x templates are large (~11 templates, dozens of columns each). Mitigation: ship the SA-CCR templates (C 34.01, C 34.02, C 34.06, C 34.07, C 34.08) first; defer IMM/CVA-only templates (C 34.03, C 34.04, C 34.10, C 34.11) until those engines exist.

#### Out-of-scope (explicit non-goals for this epic)

- **Margin model permission** ‚Äî we compute SA-CCR assuming margin agreement inputs are accurate; we do not validate or model the margin process itself.
- **CCP loss-mutualisation simulation** ‚Äî `K_CCP` is treated as a CCP-published input, not computed from the calculator.
- **Securities financing transactions exhaustive coverage** ‚Äî only those SFTs already covered by CCR Art. 271(2)(b) are in scope. Repo/reverse-repo/securities lending under comprehensive method stay in CRM (see scope decision 4 above).
- **Long-settlement transaction modelling beyond the MPOR floor** ‚Äî Art. 271(2) explicit, but bespoke modelling is not.
- **Market risk on derivatives** ‚Äî only counterparty default risk (and CVA in sub-epic). Trading book / FRTB market risk is a separate project.
